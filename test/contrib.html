MIRACLE JONES

Born in Bryan, Texas and lived in Texas for several decades before hopping on a Greyhound bus and moving to NYC because that is where "publishing is at." He has been a dishwasher, a paralegal, a cold-caller, an illegal underground sandwich-maker, a copywriter, a bartender, a barista, a copy-shop employee, a night-shift stocker, a merchandise packer, a gardener, a nanny, a video store clerk, an incense sales associate, and an author of "how-to" books about making coffee, taking care of an infant, and Bruce Lee.

He wrote this commercial!

He helped illustrate this book about Fidel Castro!

He has also written hundreds of short stories, five full-length stage plays, a handful of songs, and three novels. He is working on a fantasy/sci-fi series about psychics called "The Fold." The first two books in this series are already complete: "Sharing" and "Shifting." His first novel, "Rocks at the Cuts" was an experiment in digital publishing, coded for touch-screen tablets before touch-screen tablets even existed. You can read it here.

Jones was also the first person to publish books as "digital sculptures," a phenomenon that he predicts will be quite "the thing" in the 21st century, especially once wireless routers become small enough to fit inside everyday objects like coffee mugs, neckties, paperbacks, and 3D-printed bionic kidneys.

He published his short story collection "Seed" inside 3-inch USB dildos and his short-story collection "Soil" inside floppy USB vaginas that look like sandworms. The sculptures contained every possible ebook format, in addition to live and studio recordings of the individual short stories. The USB drives also contained a PDF book block so that you could print hard copies of "Seed" and :Soil" on an Espresso book machine at your local indie bookstore.

You can take a look at "Seed" and "Soil" here.

Jones has never been published by "outside sources," except for a magazine for people in prison called "Out of the Gutter," and by Megazine. He has received thousands of rejections for his work.

However, along with Jeanne Thornton, Anton Solomonik, Kevin Carter, and Jason Laney, Jones was a founder of the "Fiction Circus," an online literary magazine that specializes in controversial fiction, online activism, and helping literature transition between the print and digital ages, fighting against corporate censorship by behemoths such as Amazon, Google, Apple, and Paypal.

Fiction Circus

After doing extremely elaborate, unattended fiction readings in Austin for many years, the Fiction Circus started staging readings in Brooklyn and Manhattan at places like the Stain Bar and the KGB Bar.

Now, the third Friday of every month, the Fiction Circus helps Mr. Kevin Carter host "Derangement of the Senses" at the Happy Ending Lounge. This is a multimedia reading showcase that brings together the best performers in the city, featuring poetry, storytelling. fiction, essays, burlesque. and song. The motto of Derangement of the Senses is "the purpose of literature is to fuck you up!" It is a free show.

Jones tries to keep a good attitude about life, though he believes that life is truly terrifying and hard. He is diligently at work on his fourth novel and he lives in Queens.

THE BRUCE HIGH QUALITY FOUNDATION

The official arbiter of the estate of Bruce High Quality, is dedicated to the preservation of the legacy of the late social sculptor, Bruce High Quality. In the spirit of the life and work of Bruce High Quality, we aspire to invest the experience of public space with wonder, to resurrect art history from the bowels of despair, and to impregnate the institutions of art with the joy of man’s desiring.
